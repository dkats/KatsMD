Sworkit

A 2015 study at the University of Florida, identified Sworkit as most closely following the guidelines set by the American College of Sports Medicine out of a pool of 30 apps. I personally have used two of the Sworkit apps: Abs & Core and Upper Body, and I have been very happy with them.

PROs:

  • Very easy to use
  • Can pick any duration of exercise
  • Exercises are very simple, don't require any equipment
  • Short video clips show exactly what to do for each exercise
  • Each exercise only lasts 30 seconds, there are 30 second breaks sprinkled in
  • Completely free! There is a premium version that gives you a little extra, but it's absolutely unnecessary

CONs:

  • Pool of exercises is relatively small and can be repetitive. There are usually enough to avoid repeats during a 10-minute workout.
  • The exercises only have videos, not specific instructions, so you have to figure out how to get the most out of it. For example, for a "swimmer" (lying on your belly and moving your arms and legs up and down as if you're swimming), it's up to you to figure out how high to raise your arms and legs, how quickly to move, etc.

Here's a list of the apps they have:

  • Butt: iOS | Android
  • Stretching & Pilates: iOS | Android
  • Upper Body: iOS | Android
  • Lower Body: iOS | Android
  • Cardio: iOS | Android
  • Kids: iOS | Android

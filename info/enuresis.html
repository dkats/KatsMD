Bed-wetting

Nighttime bed-wetting, also called nocturnal enuresis, is quite common at up to 7 years of age, affecting as many as 10% of kids. Typically, they are very deep sleepers and soak the bed. Many children with this condition also have some degree of constipation.

We typically do not use medicines to treat nocturnal enuresis until about 9 years of age, unless the patient is very motivated to stay dry. Here are non-medical tips that can help:

  • Make sure your child has daily, soft bowel movements; you can use prune/pear/apple juice or try some Miralax
  • Limit evening fluid intake to no more than 2 oz for 2-3 hours before going to sleep (including fruit, ice cream, etc.)
  • Make sure your child urinates just before going to sleep
  • No caffeine or sugar after 5pm
  • Consider a moisture alarm. These come in auditory and vibratory models, and some do both. The auditory alarm often wakes up other family members and not the child. It might take 4 to 6 months for this to begin working.

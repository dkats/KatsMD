Breastfeeding

General

  • Skin to skin contact to increase milk supply and opportunity to breastfeed.
  • Feed on demand every 1.5-3 hours with a goal of 8-10 feeds in 24 hours. Listen for audible suck/swallow to determine feeding duration. Baby may only feed on one side per feed, but both sides should be offered.
  • Massage the breast while breastfeeding to maximize each mouthful.
  • Pull down on the baby's chin to widen the latch and make sure lips are flanged.
  • Limit pacifier use or eliminate it if possible.
  • Drink lots of fluids.
  • Foods that help increase milk supply include: oatmeal, green and orange vegetables, watermelon, dates, eggs, carrot juice, nuts (almonds and cashews), soups with lots of broth, and red meat.

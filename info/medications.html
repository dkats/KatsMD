Medications

Poison Control

800-222-1222

Must be called from a local phone (the call is automatically routed based on the area code of the phone)

If you are worried about an ingestion of a medication (or other substance), please call Poison Control as soon as possible. Do not make your child vomit. The Poison Control experts will guide you with what to do.

Safe Storage of Medications

  • Safety caps on medication bottles
  • Keep medications away from sight and reach
  • Consider locked container or cabinet
  • Remind guests to keep bags/purses with medications away from children
  • Teach children about medication safety and never call medication “candy”
  • Medication Safety Tips for Families (HealthyChildren.org, American Academy of Pediatrics)
  • Up and Away Campaign (CDC)

Tips for Giving Kids Medications

  • Make charts or other aids for scheduling meds and/or reward with sticker chart (here's an example)
  • For long term meds, tie administration to a daily activity (e.g., when you brush your teeth in the morning or evening)
  • Set phone alarms/reminders
  • Give mediation after ice cubes or popsicles to numb the taste buds
  • Change the taste! Some pharmacies carry FLAVORx, which can add flavors to medications (unfortunately, for an extra fee)

How to Swallow Pills

  • Practice with small candies and gradually increase their size (sprinkles, Tic-Tacs, M&Ms, gummies)
  • Drink plenty of water
  • Try different head positions (tilt back)
  • Try swallowing with yogurt or apple sauce
  • Learning How to Swallow a Pill (Massachusetts General Hospital)
  • PillSwallowing.com

Safe Disposal of Medications

  • Follow instructions on prescription label
  • Drug Disposal: Drug Take Back Options (FDA)
  • Some hospitals, doctor’s offices, pharmacies, and police departments have take back programs
  • Drug Disposal: FDA’s Flush List for Certain Medicines
  • Medication patches should be folded in half before disposing
  • If none of the above are options:
    • Remove all personal identification information from prescription bottles and toss in recycling/trash
    • Mix unused medication with undesirable substance (dirt, kitty litter, coffee grounds), seal in container, then toss in trash
  • How to Safely Dispose of Old Medicines (HealthyChildren.org, American Academy of Pediatrics)

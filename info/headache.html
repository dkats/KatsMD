Prevention:

  • See the Headache Hygeine section below
  • Eat breakfast and lunch every day; protein shakes are ok
  • Consider a trial of MigreLief (riboflavin (vitamin B2), Puracol Feverfew, magnesium)

Treatment:

  • Excedrin with caffeine (migraine preparation) as soon as headache starts
  • Keep record of headaches

Headache Hygeine

  • Sleep
    • Have regular sleep patterns: go to sleep and wake up at the same time every day (even on weekends)
    • Get an appropriate amount of sleep every night
      • 4-12 months: 12-16 hours, including naps
      • 1-2 years: 11-14 hours, including naps
      • 3-5 years: 10-13 hours, including naps
      • 6-12 years: 9-12 hours
      • 13-18 years: 8-10 hours
  • Exercise
    • Exercise regularly, 3-5 times per week
    • At least 30 minutes of aerobic activity
  • Hydration
    • Dehydration can both cause headaches and make existing headaches worse
    • Juice and soda do not count
    • Drink at least this much water every day:
      • 1-3 years: 3 cups
      • 4-8 years: 4 cups
      • 9-13 years: 7 cups (boys) or 6 cups (girls)
      • 14-18 years: 9 cups (boys) or 7 cups (girls)
      • Adult: 11 cups (boys) or 7 cups (girls)
  • Meals
    • Eat 3-5 meals per day
    • No skipping meals
    • Stay away from fast foods, junk food, and processed foods
  • Stress
    • Limit stress by avoiding conflicts
    • Try relaxation techniques: bio-feedback, “stress breaks”, yoga, meditation, talk therapy
    • Consider cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT)
  • Triggers
    • Pass up common food triggers (processed foods, junk food, caffeine)
    • Don't take Tylenol/Motrin/Advil/Aleve more than 3 times per week

Headache Diary

Keeping a headache diary can help your doctor make an accurate diagnosis and develop an effective treatment plan. For each headache, write down the following:

  • Date
  • Time
  • Location on head
  • Throbbing?
  • Intensity (1-10)
  • What were you doing when the headache started?
  • Nausea/Vomiting?
  • What helped the headache go away? (e.g. sleep, quiet, Tylenol, ibuprofen)
  • Duration

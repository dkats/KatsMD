Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs) / Diseases (STDs)

Please note: a few of these disease are not STIs/STDs, but I included them here, because they are genital diseases. These are clearly marked ("Not an STI"), so that you won't mix them up.

The point of this page is not to teach you the specifics about each STI so that you can diagnose them. The point is to learn when to go see a doctor, what happens when you get an STI, and how to keep yourself from getting one in the first place.

STIs vs. STDs

What's the difference between a sexually transmitted infection (STI) and a sexually transmitted disease (STD)? "Infection" means there's a bacteria, virus, fungus, or parasite living in/on your body that shouldn't normally be there. "Disease" means something happened to your body to make you feel sick. So, what's the difference? You can have an infection without feeling sick. In fact, most people with an STI feel completely fine and don't know that they have an infection.
